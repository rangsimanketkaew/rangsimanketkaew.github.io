Short story from the Berlin Marathon 2023 🇩🇪

🏃🏻‍♂️ Last Sunday, September 24, I ran a marathon at the Berlin Marathon. This was the 49th time and there were 
a lot of people participating (almost 50,000 people) because the Berlin Maraton is one of the six world major 
marathons.

🏃🏻‍♂️ I went racing with the adidas Runners Zurich (AR Zurich) which sponsored a bib for me 😜 I knew that 
I would be running in Berlin about a month and a half before the racing day, so I had very little time to 
prepare but I intend to prepare as much as I can. Because it is not so easy to run on a world major course, 
and importantly, this is my first marathon, so I want to do my best.

🏃🏻‍♂️ I ran in the wave 1, which was the first wave released, but I was in group D, which is the last group of wave 1. 
The start time of wave 1 is exactly at 9:15 a.m. (Kipchoge was also in this wave in the front row), 
but I actually started running from the starting point around 9:30 a.m.!! In this long run I listened to the 
song because, I personally like to listen to dancing, fast, exciting music. It helps make me feel good 🤣 
Normally, I listen to both international and Thai music, but I much more like Thai country dance songs that 
are remixed more. If you're interested in the playlist, check it out below!.

🏃🏻‍♂️ there were a lot of people in the first 10 kilometers. It was almost impossible to run very fast because 
there were so many people trying to find their own space to run fast. It was difficult for me to overtake and 
I had to be careful as I did not want to trip or cause others to fall. After the first 10 kilometers 
the traffic picked up a bit and I had more space to run. My goal was to finish my first marathon in 3 hours 
20 minutes (aim too high, probably 😅), but when my actual run was not what I expected...

🏃🏻‍♂️ During the first 20 kilometers (First half marathon), I achieved the desired speed, which was a pace of 
about 4.30 min/km. But at the start of the kilometer 21, I felt a strange sensation in the back of my left thigh 
(Hamstring), which I knew immediately that it was cramps!! There was nothing I could do except slow down or 
stop running. What I did was to reduce my speed and walk during the water intake from time to time, 
which caused my pace to 5.30-6.00 min/km and then I had to keep running until I reached the finish line. 
Ultimately, I did the 42-km run in 3 hours and 42 minutes 😇

🏃🏻‍♂️ Asked if I was disappointed, I was a bit disappointed because the running didn't go as expected. 
I know that I still lack experience; either taking a glass of water or finding a moment to take over other 
runners in front of me. I also didn't plan on whether to run a positive split or a negative split. 
This is my first marathon, so I wanted to do my best. But everything has both pros and cons. 
During the second half where I had to run slower, I had more time to focus on the supporters on the sidelines. 
It made me very happy and I could feel the strength throughout the run which helped me reach the finish line in the end 😊

The event was very well organized. There are a lot of officers and they provide crystal clear information, 
also first aid units and security units. There are drinking water and energy drink stations for you along the way 
(every 5 km) and there are fruits such as bananas and apples as well.
